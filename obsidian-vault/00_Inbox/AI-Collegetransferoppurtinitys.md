# AI Summary — College Transfer Opportunities

**Question:** Which colleges should I apply to transfer into (originally: besides A&M), and how do I navigate the calculus prerequisite and cost constraints along the way?

**Answer:**

- **Core blocker:** You're locked out of the Precalc→Calc I→Calc II sequence, ruling out McCombs, Michigan Ross, Baylor, NYU Stern, and Georgetown (all require real calculus, not Business Calc).
- **Final list, Business Calc-compatible:**
    - Texas A&M Mays — primary anchor, in-state (~$32-33K/year), affordable and close to your current TTU cost
    - UT Austin Economics — no calc prerequisite to apply, take calc in-residence after enrolling, in-state cost
    - SMU Cox — secondary anchor; automatic transfer merit scholarship (up to $25K/year for 3.7+ GPA) brings net cost to ~$54,660/year living at home in Flower Mound
    - TCU Neeley — solid target, accepts Business Calc
    - UT Dallas Jindal — safety, in-state, accepts Business Calc
    - Indiana Kelley — long-shot reach, ~$57,600/year sticker, best scholarship odds of the reaches
    - UNC Kenan-Flagler — long-shot reach, ~$59-60K/year sticker, weaker scholarship odds
- **Ruled out:** UVA McIntire (too expensive without aid, no in-state path since your dad's domicile stayed Texas and he's moving back); Georgetown/American/George Mason/GW (DC-area proximity to your dad no longer relevant once he's headed back to Texas).

**Goals going forward:**

1. Keep grades locked in through Fall 2026/Spring 2027 — GPA drives both admission and scholarship eligibility everywhere on this list.
2. Track how Summer 2027 S&T recruiting plays out at TTU before treating any transfer as a must — those outcomes land before you'd even transfer.
3. Submit UNC first (~Feb 15, 2027 deadline), then Mays/UVA-tier deadlines (March 1), then SMU/Kelley/TCU (June 1).
4. Treat Kelley and UNC as your two intentional long shots; treat Mays, SMU, UT Austin Econ, TCU, and UTD as your realistic, most likely outcomes.
