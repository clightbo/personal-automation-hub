Here's a summary of everything we've worked through:

Build toward a **Sales & Trading seat** (trading side specifically, not client-facing sales) as an early-career step, with a **longer-term goal of asset management or running a macro fund by ~30**. S&T is viewed as a stepping stone — not the final destination — followed by equity research/AM experience to build thesis-construction skills before eventually managing capital independently.

### Key Questions Worked Through

**SEO Career form** — Selected **Sales & Trading** as the interest area, since it's the most direct match to your options-trading background and long-term goal, versus Investment Management, Investment Research, or Wealth Management (ruled out as too relationship/sales-driven).

**Can I actually do this?** — Explored fears around not being "smart enough" and not being a morning person. Conclusion: your track record (4.0, competitive selections, self-built trading bots) shows genuine capability; morning hours are a trainable habit, not a fixed trait.

**Will I like it / can I handle losing?** — You identified real signals that trading suits you: you get absorbed in it (time passes quickly), and — most importantly — you can follow your rules again after a loss rather than revenge-trading. That emotional recovery pattern is one of the strongest predictors of lasting on a desk.

**Do I still pursue AM and Equity Research too?** — Yes, but sequenced _after_ trading, not instead of it. Trading builds real-time execution/risk instincts; research builds thesis-construction skills; AM is the eventual destination. All three tracks are still worth pursuing prep for (Wall Street Prep modeling, Akuna Options 101, REIT pitch).

Current resume rated 9/10, with real S&T (8.5), Equity Research (8), and AM (7.5) fit — all strong for a rising sophomore.
